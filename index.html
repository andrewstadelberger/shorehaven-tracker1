The topics we built — question bank

Seven topics for Mike & Mike, with questions for each. ★ = the 15 priority must-asks. Edit freely today.

1 World Events & The Markets

Separate "headline noise" from the things that actually move a portfolio.
  1. ★ When a big headline hits — a war, an election, a tariff — what's the first thing you tell clients who call you that morning?
  2. How do you tell the difference between a headline that's noise vs. a real shift in the landscape?
  3. Are there world events right now you think investors are under-reacting to?
  4. ★ What's the biggest mistake you see regular investors make when the news cycle gets loud?
  5. If someone feels anxious every time they open their phone, what would you tell them to do this week?

2 Inflation

Turn inflation from an abstract number into "what it means for my money."
  1. ★ In plain English, where does inflation stand right now vs. where the Fed wants it?
  2. ★ For a diversified portfolio, what tends to hold up best when inflation runs hot?
  3. People hear "sticky inflation" — what does that actually mean for a retirement plan?
  4. How should someone think about cash right now? Is holding it costing them more than they realize?
  5. ★ If a client came in today worried inflation is eating their savings, what's the conversation you'd have?

3 Gold & Silver

Demystify precious metals — a portfolio tool, not a doomsday play.
  1. ★ Gold's been on a historic run. What's driving it, and is it too late for a regular investor to participate?
  2. What's the case for silver vs. gold right now — same story or different?
  3. How do you actually own gold — ETFs, physical, mining stocks? What do you recommend for most clients?
  4. Is there a "right" percentage of a portfolio to hold in metals, or does it depend on the person?
  5. ★ What's the biggest myth about gold you wish would go away?

4 Bitcoin

Treat Bitcoin as a legitimate asset class — no hype, no dismissal.
  1. ★ A few years ago, most advisors wouldn't touch Bitcoin. What changed?
  2. With spot ETFs widely available, how are you thinking about Bitcoin inside a real client portfolio?
  3. How do you answer the client who says, "just put 10% of my money in Bitcoin"?
  4. What's the difference between "I believe in Bitcoin long-term" and "I should own Bitcoin"?
  5. ★ For someone who's never owned crypto but feels like they're missing out, what's the responsible first step?
  6. How do you think about Bitcoin's volatility — avoid it, size it smaller, or something else?

5 Tokenization

The topic most viewers won't know yet — a simple explainer and a reason to care.
  1. ★ Explain tokenization like I'm a smart person who's never heard the word. What is it?
  2. Why are BlackRock, Fidelity and the big banks suddenly talking about tokenized assets?
  3. What could get tokenized that would actually matter to a regular investor — real estate, bonds, private funds?
  4. Is tokenization the same conversation as crypto, or totally separate?
  5. ★ If this plays out the way believers think, what changes for a regular investor in five years?
  6. What should someone be skeptical of when they hear "tokenization"?

6 Job Losses Due to AI

The most personal topic — connect it back to financial planning.
  1. ★ When a client worries their industry will get disrupted by AI, what's the first question you ask them?
  2. How should someone adjust their plan if there's a real chance they're displaced in five years?
  3. What's the right size of an emergency fund in an AI-disruption era? Is "3–6 months" still enough?
  4. Are there sectors or company types you're leaning into or pulling back from because of AI?
  5. ★ For a parent worried about their kid's career path, what would you tell them?
  6. Is there an opportunity side to this story people aren't talking about enough?

7 Changing Landscapes — Big-Picture Wrap

Pull it all together. The "evergreen" clips that age well.
  1. ★ Name the two or three biggest shifts in investing right now that the average person is sleeping on.
  2. What's something you believed about investing five years ago that you've updated on?
  3. People feel the rules of money are being rewritten — gold, crypto, AI, inflation all at once. Are they right, or is this just how every era feels?
  4. ★ If a viewer remembers one thing from this whole conversation, what do you want it to be?
  5. What's the question clients should be asking you right now that they aren't?
Also worth grabbing while we're rolling: advisor succession planning (the questions a client should ask), a 1-minute portfolio-update video, and a quick "staying calm at market highs" piece for the carousel.
